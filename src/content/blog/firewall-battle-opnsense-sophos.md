For many years, the firewall choice in the home lab community was almost binary. If you wanted maximum performance and full control, OPNsense was the clear favorite. Sophos Home, while visually appealing, was held back by its strict 6 GB RAM limitation.

That limitation is now gone.

### The Big Change from Sophos

In 2026, Sophos Home lifted its famous RAM cap. This opens the door for proper use of resource-heavy features such as Deep Packet Inspection (DPI), advanced Intrusion Prevention (IPS), and comprehensive threat intelligence.

**Strengths of Sophos Home in 2026:**
- Modern, clean, and very polished user interface
- Excellent out-of-the-box web filtering and application control
- Strong and visually appealing reporting and traffic analytics
- Good integration with Sophos’ global threat intelligence

### The Enduring Strengths of OPNsense

OPNsense remains extremely popular — and for good reason. As a fully open-source platform, it continues to offer a level of freedom and transparency that commercial solutions struggle to match.

**Key advantages of OPNsense:**
- Vast plugin ecosystem (WireGuard, CrowdSec, Unbound, HAProxy, and many more)
- Complete independence — no cloud account or subscription required
- Excellent hardware support and strong performance even on modest devices
- Deep customizability and full control over every aspect of the system
- Highly active community development

### Final Thoughts

The gap between the two solutions has narrowed considerably in 2026. Sophos Home is now a much more serious contender for users who value a refined, modern interface and easier management. OPNsense continues to shine strongly for those who prioritize openness, flexibility, and complete independence from any vendor.

There is no single winner — the best choice depends entirely on your personal priorities and how you want to operate your network.
